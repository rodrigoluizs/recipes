method:
  - Fit the flat beater. Cream the [[unsalted butter]], [[light brown sugar]] and [[granulated sugar]] on Speed 2 for 30 seconds, then Speed 4 for 3-4 minutes, until light, fluffy and noticeably paler.
  - Add the [[egg]] and [[vanilla extract]] and mix on Speed 2 for 30-45 seconds, just until incorporated.
  - In a separate bowl, whisk together the [[all-purpose flour]], [[cocoa powder]], [[baking soda]] and [[salt]].
  - On Speed 1, add the dry ingredients in three additions, mixing only until the flour disappears. Do not overmix.
  - Add the [[dark chocolate]] and [[milk chocolate]] and mix on Speed 1 for 10-15 seconds. Leave some larger chunks whole.
  - Weigh 40 g of dough per cookie, roll into balls, then flatten slightly to about 1.5-2 cm thick. Place on a tray lined with baking paper.
  - Chill in the fridge for at least 2 hours, ideally overnight (12-24 hours).
  - Preheat the oven to 180C (top & bottom heat). Bake for 9-11 minutes, until the edges are set, the tops have cracked and the centres still look slightly soft. Do not wait until they look completely baked.
  - Rest the cookies on the tray for 10 minutes, then transfer to a cooling rack to cool completely.

Chopping chocolate bars rather than using chips leaves pockets of melted
chocolate throughout the cookies. Baking soda (Natron) gives a chewier crumb and
better browning than baking powder, and real butter beats margarine on flavour
and texture. Sprinkle flaky sea salt over the cookies the moment they come out
of the oven for a bakery-style finish.

**KitchenAid 5KSM55SXXE (5.2 L Bowl-Lift):** use the flat beater throughout.
Speed 4 only for creaming the butter and sugars, Speed 2 for combining, and
Speed 1 when adding the dry ingredients and chocolate to avoid overmixing and
cocoa dust. Stop once to scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl.

**Convection (Umluft):** reduce the oven to 170C and start checking at 8-9
minutes.

**Storage:** airtight at room temperature for up to 5 days. Baked cookies freeze
for up to 3 months. Portioned dough balls also freeze for up to 3 months — bake
straight from frozen, adding 1-2 minutes.
