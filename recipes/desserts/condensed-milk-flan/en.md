    name: sweetened condensed milk
    note: 2 cans, 380 g each
  - amount: 520
    unit: ml
    name: whole milk
    note: about 1.5 cans, a finger short of full
  - amount: 6
    name: eggs
    note: large
  - amount: 1
    unit: tbsp
    name: dulce de leche
    note: heaping
  - amount: 225
    unit: g
    name: granulated sugar
    note: for the caramel, about 1 cup + 2 tbsp
  - amount: 150
    unit: ml
    name: hot water
    note: for the caramel, about ½ cup + 2 tbsp
method:
  - "Make the caramel: put the [[granulated sugar]] in a saucepan over medium heat. Don't stir at the start; only swirl the pan if needed, until it turns light amber — not too dark, or it will taste bitter and set too hard."
  - Carefully add the [[hot water]] and stir until you have a smooth caramel.
  - Pour the caramel into the flan pan, coating the bottom and sides. Let it cool completely before adding the flan mixture.
  - Separate the egg yolks from the whites. Sieve the yolks one by one without scraping the sieve, which removes the eggy taste, then recombine with the whites.
  - "Blend the [[eggs]] with the [[sweetened condensed milk]], [[whole milk]] and [[dulce de leche]] for only 20-30 seconds: do not overblend, or you'll trap air in the mixture."
  - Rest the mixture for 20 minutes, then skim the foam off the top with a spoon, or gently dab it with a paper towel, until the surface looks smooth. For extra smoothness, pass it through a fine sieve before pouring it into the pan.
  - Pour the mixture slowly down the side of the pan, not into the middle, to avoid creating new bubbles.
  - Cover the pan tightly with aluminum foil — a good seal matters more than which side faces up.
  - Set the pan in a water bath with hot water halfway up its sides, and don't let it dry out in the oven.
  - "Bake at 140°C (285°F) with no fan: preheat for 10-15 minutes, then bake covered for 1 hr 30 min. If the center is still too loose, add 15 more minutes, then up to another 10-15 minutes if it's still needed. Avoid the fan except as a last resort, and only briefly at the very end."
  - Turn off the oven and leave the flan inside, foil removed, for another 20-30 minutes.
  - "Check for doneness: the edges should be firm and the center should wobble gently like jelly, the top should be smooth with no cracks, and a skewer should come out moist with no raw liquid."
  - Let cool, then refrigerate for at least 6 hours, ideally overnight. It firms up a lot even if it looks soft while still warm.
  - To unmold, run a thin knife around the edge, warm the bottom of the pan briefly over low heat, then invert onto a larger plate.

A classic Brazilian flan, aimed at a smooth, hole-free custard: plenty of
condensed milk flavor, a spoonful of dulce de leche for depth, and a light
caramel underneath.

## Tips for a smooth, hole-free flan

- Sieving the yolks removes the eggy taste that can otherwise come through.
- Blend briefly (20-30 seconds); overblending whips air into the mixture.
- The 20-minute rest lets trapped bubbles rise to the surface before baking.
- Skimming the foam and pouring down the side of the pan both help avoid
  surface bubbles and holes.
- Bake low and slow, without the fan, in a water bath, for the gentlest, most
  even cooking.
- Chill thoroughly. A flan that still looks soft warm will firm up nicely
  after several hours in the fridge.
- For the caramel, stop as soon as it's light amber. Too dark and it turns
  bitter and sets rock hard instead of staying pourable once cool.
