A same-day or overnight Neapolitan dough scaled for a KitchenAid stand mixer.
The long, cool fermentation with just 1 g of dry yeast is what builds the flavour
and the airy, blistered cornicione.

**Timing.** Beyond the 30 min of active prep, plan for a 2 hr first rise and a
second rise of 2–4 hr (same day) or overnight in the fridge plus 4–5 hr at room
temperature. Total time is roughly 5–7 hours the same day, or spread over a night.

## Equipment

- KitchenAid 5KSM55SXXE (KSM55) bowl-lift stand mixer with the dough hook
- Large proofing container with a lid
- Bench scraper and kitchen scale
- Pizza peel and a pizza stone or steel (recommended)
- Pizza cutter

## KitchenAid notes

- Use the dough hook throughout mixing and kneading.
- Keep the mixer at Speed 1–2 only when making yeast dough.
- The 5.2 L bowl handles this 1 kg flour batch comfortably.
- If the dough seems dry in the first few minutes, let the mixer keep working
  before adding extra water — "00" flour absorbs moisture gradually.

## Storage

- Dough balls keep in the fridge for up to 48 hours, which improves the flavour.
- Bring refrigerated dough back to room temperature for 4–5 hours before stretching.
- Baked pizza is best eaten immediately, but keeps for up to 3 days refrigerated;
  reheat on a hot skillet or pizza stone.

## Tips

- A high-protein "00" pizza flour gives the most authentic Neapolitan texture.
- Using only 1 g of dry yeast drives a slow fermentation that develops excellent flavour.
- Handle the dough gently after proofing to preserve the air bubbles.
- A pizza steel usually beats a stone in a home oven — it transfers heat more efficiently.
- Use fresh mozzarella that has been well drained to keep the pizza from going soggy.
