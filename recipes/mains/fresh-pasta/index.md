---
title: Fresh Pasta
servings: 5
prepTime: 30 min
cookTime: 3 min
ingredients:
  - amount: 5
    name: eggs
    note: organic
  - amount: 300
    unit: g
    name: all-purpose flour
  - amount: 200
    unit: g
    name: fine semolina flour
  - name: water
    note: as needed
method:
  - Tip the [[all-purpose flour]] and [[fine semolina flour]] onto a work surface and mix until evenly blended.
  - Shape the flour into a mound and hollow out a wide well in the center, then crack the [[eggs]] into the well.
  - Whisk the [[eggs]] with a fork, keeping the walls of the well intact so they don't run out.
  - Gradually pull flour from the inner edges of the well into the eggs, incorporating a little at a time.
  - Bring everything together with your hands, using a bench scraper to gather any loose pieces.
  - Knead for about 10 minutes with a push-and-fold motion, rotating the dough as you go, until it is smooth, elastic, and well combined.
  - "Adjust the moisture as needed: dampen your hands slightly if the dough is dry, or add a little [[water]] if dry flour remains; if it's sticky, dust with more flour."
  - Wrap the dough tightly in plastic wrap or cover it with an overturned bowl and let it rest for 10–15 minutes at room temperature before rolling and shaping.

A classic egg-and-semolina fresh pasta dough. The semolina gives it a firmer
bite and helps it hold its shape, while the eggs make it rich and golden.

Once rested, roll it out (by hand or with a pasta machine) and cut into your
shape of choice — tagliatelle, fettuccine, lasagne sheets, or filled pasta.
Fresh pasta cooks fast: drop it into well-salted boiling water for just 2–3
minutes.
