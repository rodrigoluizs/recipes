---
title: Cheese Bread (Pão de Queijo)
servings: 25
prepTime: 40 min
cookTime: 25–30 min
ingredients:
  - amount: 250
    unit: g
    name: semi-cured cheese
    note: grated (about 2 cups)
  - amount: 85
    unit: g
    name: parmesan cheese
    note: grated (about ½ cup)
  - amount: 50
    unit: g
    name: oil
    note: about 4 tbsp
  - amount: 200
    unit: g
    name: milk
    note: about 1 cup
  - amount: 250
    unit: g
    name: sour cassava starch
    note: polvilho azedo (about 2 cups)
  - amount: 85
    unit: g
    name: sweet cassava starch
    note: polvilho doce (about ½ cup)
  - amount: 6
    unit: g
    name: salt
    note: or two generous pinches
  - amount: 2
    name: egg
    note: whole
method:
  - In a pot, heat the [[milk]], [[oil]] and [[salt]] until it starts to boil.
  - Place the starches ([[sour cassava starch]] and [[sweet cassava starch]]) in a large bowl and pour the hot mixture on top, mixing well to scald.
  - Let the dough cool until lukewarm or nearly cold — if too hot, the [[egg]] will cook when added.
  - Add the [[egg]] and mix just enough to incorporate — mixing too much with only the egg makes the dough too sticky.
  - Add the grated [[semi-cured cheese]] and [[parmesan cheese]] right after and mix well until you get a smooth, homogeneous dough.
  - Shape into 25 g balls, weighed on a scale to keep them uniform and ensure they bake evenly.
  - Preheat the oven to 190 °C (375 °F).
  - Place on a baking tray lined with parchment paper, leaving space between the rolls.
  - Bake at 190 °C for 25 to 30 minutes, or until lightly golden.
  - Serve hot.

tags: [brazilian, cheese, snack, gluten-free]
---

**Tip:** the dough can be frozen already shaped — bake directly from frozen, adding 5 minutes to the baking time.
